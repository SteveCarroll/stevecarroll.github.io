neverheardof'im, so I read the articles on the book, which sounded more
critical than 'best selling', and his article, which was much more
positive about his work, and then since I was bemused by the concept of
his book having been turned into a 'graphic novel', I had to read that

to be 'speculative' and 'sensational'.

In my schoolboy days, I had access to all the comic books and magazines
I could read, so I read them 'all' or nearly all; women's mags, men's
mags, craft mags, hunting & fishing mags, sexology mag, and I could have
all of the comic books w/ their covers torn off.

A seller of comic books and mags can get credit for the unsold merch
simply by returning the top part of the front cover while discarding the
rest, so I got to take home those coverless comics. So I've seen a LOT
of comics back in my day, but no graphic novels, much less a graphic
'textbook' of 'popular science'.
