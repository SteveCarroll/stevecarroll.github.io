AF (b 1929) & her sister Margot died in 1945 of (epidemic) typhus in
Bergen-Belsen camp along w/ the deaths of 35000 others consisting of
Soviet POWs & Jews. It was liberated about 2 mo later by Brit soldiers
who found about 60000 inside & another 13000 corpses lying around unburied.

For those who are more familiar w/ the other camps, the wp article on
B-B is interesting, as its purpose evolved from 1935 to its liberation.
No gas chambers at B-B but at its satellites.

Epidemic typhus is transmitted by human lice, as opposed to
some other typhus spread by rat fleas. The organism is Rickettsia
prowazekii; untreated the mortality rate is about 40%. During WW II we
dev'd vaccine against the org for our soldiers and infection is now easily
treated by a common antibiotic tetracycline which was dev'd later in 1953.

I haven't yet found the answer to what antibiotic treatment might've
been used in WW II, as penicillin isn't effective.

The org is an obligate parasite, meaning that it can't support itself
but depends on its metabolism from living inside another's cells. After
infection, the louse dies less than a week later because the Rp has
infected its gut lining cells. Humans in bad living conditions w/ these
lice get the louse feces into skin abrasions, and the organism actually
remains infective a while in dead louse feces.

Reading about how untreated Rp kills is disheartening, but the process
leads to multi-organ system failure.

I found a nice article considering Rp as a wartime problem as the
article has good dev of what all happened before and during WWII, but it
dev/s the story of the 'crude' vaccine dev, not any antibiotic mention.
