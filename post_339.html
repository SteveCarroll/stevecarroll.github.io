In case you are wondering if SJ knew he had pancreatic cancer(usually a
very lethal disease), when he said 'your time is limited', the answer is
'yes'.

He said it at a commencement in '05, he was dx/ed in '03.

SJ's rare type of pancreatic cancer was badly mismanaged by him. It is
impossible to say if he might've survived it better if he had taken care
of the problem conventionally. In any case, he was dead at 56, having
delayed conventional management for 9 mo, but living 8 y after dx,
including getting a liver transplant besides the delayed conventional
surgery.

The 'experts' disagree wildly, some say he only mildly decreased his
chance of survival by his initial management choices, while others say
that cancer would have been expected to be curable managed
conventionally instead of the 9 mo of crazy alternative ideas.

Personally, I think his initial choices were ridiculous, but I suspect
things would've turned out the same even if he had made the proper
choice from the gitgo.
