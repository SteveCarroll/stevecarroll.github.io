When the leeches and night crawlers arrived at the party, do you suppose
they were given a worm welcome?

Solving: when the, night, they were given, welcome

Worm reproduction in earthworms and leeches is hermaphroditic. The
little band (clitellum) you see on an earthworm is a viscid reproductive
sac for eggs, but you can't usually see it on a leech unless it is full
of eggs.

As to reproductive strategies, 94% of flowering plants are
hermaphroditic. The discussion of hermaphroditism in its wp article has
some interesting bits, such as 'sequential'.

There is also fodder to be learned in the article on evolution of sexual
reproduction.
