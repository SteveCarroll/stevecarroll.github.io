Failure is simply the opportunity to begin again. This time more
intelligently.
Henry Ford

Solving: 'the' 'henry' 'opportunity to' 'intelligently'

HF didn't begin again his failed idea for a rubber plantation in Brazil
at Fordlandia. Brazil gave him 2.5 mil acres and no taxes in exchange
for 9% of the profits. Starting in 1926 the town was founded by 1928,
but wasn't abandoned until 1934. Well, I guess you could call the move
to Belterra a form of beginning again and that was a viable community,
but it wasn't until the 40s that production became economically
feasible, and by then synthetic rubber had arrived. When I was looking
around for more info on the Fordlandia failure, I found a more complete
story at
https://www.thehenryford.org/collections-and-research/digital-resources/popular-topics/brazilian-rubber-plantations/

Apparently TheHenryFord site is the digital 'branch' of the history
museum complex in Dearborn.
