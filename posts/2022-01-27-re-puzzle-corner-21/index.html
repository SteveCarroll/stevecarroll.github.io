And on another note about linux…

I ran into an article today that might be of interest to those ‘sometime’ linux users. First, I think the site, known as MUO, makeuseof, is an interesting site, byline ’technology simplified’. The writer of the article has put out several articles which might be of interest to those Win & Mac users who aren’t at all interested in changing their OS but are among the ’linux curious’.

I think linux curious is just fine. Personally I think the best way to be linux curious is to try out a LOT of linux distro/s as live boots, usually w/o applying persistence. I think the best way to do that now is using a Ventoy USB stick but there are other ways.

In any case, this particular article was about several different DEs, desktop environments, which I think is a valuable concept to the linux curious because it is so different from the Mac & Win world in which the user is provided the MS or Apple result which is tweakable.

The article I’m referring to is titled The 12 Best Linux Desktop Environments and as a general rule, The X best Xs articles are usually just clickbait; and I don’t think ‘best’ is the operative word here. It could have been titled, ‘a /very/ few words about several linux DEs’.
