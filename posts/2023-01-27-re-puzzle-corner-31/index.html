Good friends, good books and a sleepy conscience: this is the ideal life. Mark Twain

Solving: ‘good’ ’this is the’ ‘friends’ ‘ideal life’

While I was looking around to see if anyone explained just what he had in mind w/ ‘sleepy conscience’ I ran into a rather dumb explanation which mentioned that the quote also appeared in the ‘album’ (?) of Archduchess Marie Therese (of Austria) which led me to some more reading about the some 20 months he spent in Vienna which led to some more reading about whereall he traveled/lived. It seems like a strange life to me. Traveling is one thing; living there is another thing; MT seemed to do something ’else’; and his wife and children w/ him, for quite a number of years.

It seems that much of the time he didn’t really have a ‘home’ in the conventional sense, but he had a family. Seems strange.
