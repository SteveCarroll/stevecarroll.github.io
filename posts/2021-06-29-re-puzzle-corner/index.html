June 29, 2021

Today’s CQ:

Obsessed by a fairy tale, we spend our lives searching for a magic door and a lost kingdom of peace. Eugene O’Neill

As is so often the case, I found myself casting about to see if there were some useful commentary on some context or other clue to what EON was talking about there.

One little clue said, ‘Stately Mansions’ 1964

Huh? 1964?!

So I went to see if the wp could tell me anything about that. Sure enough, it was something unfinished that EON did in ‘36-‘39 as a sequel that he did NOT want pub’d posthumously. But his widow did anyway, so it came out in ‘64.

In order to get the whole story, one has to read the article on EON, and A Touch of the Poet, and More Stately Mansions, of which the article on him is most interesting. His dying words, “I knew it. I knew it. Born in a hotel room and died in a hotel room.”

Nobel in Literature ‘36; 4 Pulitzers in Drama

On another note; a site w/ multiple facets, DistroWatch puts out a weekly column which itself has multiple parts. This week’s had an interesting article which was written in reply to a question to give the answer to ‘Why there are so many Linux shells’.

That section was interesting, but even more so was one of the reader comments who felt that the web was full of ’trash concerning Bash’ so he provided some Bash resources which he felt were good.
