If you go back to her earliest name, Mary Anne Evans, evolved different varieties of that name and marriage (at the end of her life) made it Mary Ann Cross.

But that ’education’ was only to age 16. (And/But) Her father was manager of an estate of a family of wealth.

Her wp article explains quite a lot of her life and evolution as a writer. and eventually…

By the time she was 37, she had her first work as GE pub’d.

Skipping past her literary career, I was ‘bemused’ by the story of her marriage. In May 1880 at age 60 she married John Cross, who was 40. On their honeymoon in Venice in an alleged suicide attempt he jumped from a hotel balcony into the Grand Canal, but survived. She, however, took ill from a throat infection and due to other complications of her health, died the same year she married, back in Chelsea.

Naturally her literary productivity is more important than the tidbits I mention here which I found titillating.
