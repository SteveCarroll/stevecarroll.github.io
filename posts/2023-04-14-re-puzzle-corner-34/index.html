Mike Easter

April 14, 2023

Today’s CQ:

KMFQ SHOU UKUN XQU LEU MVBK BHAEL H JVMS YQMW UPLHVAFHNEUO RMVNLUBBXLHMVN. – CXIBM VUQFOX

. . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . .

Your wide eyes are the only light I know from extinguished constellations. Pablo Neruda

Solving: Very interesting.  I really wanted U>E so much that I wouldn’t let it go even tho’ it wasn’t working out because of that K.  For the longest, I tho’t if U were E, then UKUN had to be ’ever’ making the first word start w/ V and some word end w/ V.  Bad news.  Finally, I tried every letter in the alphabet for the K and realized I hadn’t tho’t of Y, so ’ever’ could be ’eyes’ and the first word could be ‘your’ and then I was going somewhere.

Part of the reason I was holding on to the U>E was because even tho’ XQU wasn’t ’the’, LEU /could/ be, and that would suit the ‘-ight’ word just fine, so I was very sure that LEU was ’the’.

I don’t really like that kind of poetry that Neruda does; here’s the rest of that one.

I love the handful of the earth you are. Because of its meadows, vast as a planet, I have no other star. You are my replica of the multiplying universe.

Your wide eyes are the only light I know from extinguished constellations; your skin throbs like the streak of a meteor through rain.

Your hips were that much of the moon for me; your deep mouth and its delights, that much sun; your heart, fiery with its long red rays,

was that much ardent light, like honey in the shade. So I pass across your burning form, kissing you - compact and planetary, my dove, my globe.

But, I did like PN’s wp article.

– Mike Easter
