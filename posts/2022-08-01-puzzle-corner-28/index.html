Today’s CQ:

  QXKM ECOSKSF, QXV MYS   VSRYOVM IZMQ RZJS. K   OVZBKMV QXZQ KQ KM   ZYFYMQ: QXV MYEEVO’M BZMQ   MQZSR. – MZOZ HZYEV

. . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . .

This morning, the sun endures past dawn. I realise that it is August: the summer’s last stand. Sara Baume

Solving: ‘it is’ ’this’ ’the’ ’that’ ‘summer’s’. Last words: ‘past’ ‘dawn’ & ‘Baume’ because of single uses of P, W, & B.

And, I don’t know what ’the sun endures past dawn’ is supposed to mean yet.  My tho’ts this morning were ’the sun is coming up later now’. Not ‘poetic’ or quotable.

Interestingly SB only knows approximately how old she is, born in 1984, apparently unrecorded.  Some of my relatives recorded birth dates and such on a page in a Bible.  My mother was born ‘at home’ in a ranch house, but she knew when it was and her birth was officially recorded, ie she had a birth certificate. I call it a ‘ranch’ because there were some animals there, chickens, cows, horses, pigs and no crops; so it wasn’t a ‘farm house’. Also no nearby neighbors. She called it the ‘home place’.

Oh, I forgot; and a GREAT dog. I lived there for a while as a child. Two different dogs, a red chow and a German shepherd. My red chow had a longer nose than the wp pix of chow chows. More like the b&w one of Red Craze, except she wasn’t as big as that.

My first dogs took care of me, not the other way around. Particularly that German shepherd.

– Mike Easter
