Solving: ‘people who’ ’think they know everything’

More than half a dozen words were reorganized by the online OCR.  I’m still baffled by how that algo is written in some vain attempt to do/perform/say something or other that it should just stay out of and perform just its proper character recognition business. When it messes up so much, it would be quicker to do it manually; alternatively use some other tool.  You can’t really tell how much time you are going to waste on the rearranging until you are in the middle of it and might as well finish that way.  It is like having an ‘able assistant’ who drops the ball every once in a while which requires doing his job all over again.  You can’t decide whether to fire him or keep doing it the same way.

That’s an interesting quote by IA; he was a very smart multi-talented ‘genius’ who knew a lot about a lot of things; and since he was (also) a uni prof, he probably ran into a lot of people who tho’t they were pretty smart, but who weren’t nearly as smart as he was.  I’ve been around some of those environments, seeing very smart people run into really smart people; and seeing very hard-working and smart people who run into really hard-working who are also really smart people.  They are phenomenal.

(But) It is a lot easier to be kinda smart and fairly lazy :-)
