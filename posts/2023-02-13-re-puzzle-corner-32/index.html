Mike Easter

February 13, 2023

Today’s CQ:

OR KRDV NME UV KRDVE BG OR AVVK OCV GLM AXRP UROC GBEVG. – ENDBE DBGJROO . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . .

To love and be loved is to feel the sun from both sides. - David Viscott

Solving: ’the’ ’to’ ‘be’ ‘both’ ‘from both sides’

Personally, I don’t ‘appreciate’ such a quote. It is supposed to be ‘romantic’, but to me it just seems ‘stupid’. I can ‘get’ ‘Love is grand’ or somesuch simple, but when ‘poets’ or whatever you call that kind of language start creating stupid metaphors, they ’lose’ me.

For example; depending on the situation, it isn’t always a positive to feel the heat/rays of the sun; sometimes it is brutally painful and blistering. Picture someone dying in the desert sun. I’m sure that isn’t what DV had in mind. And as another example, astronomically speaking, a habitable planet/world w/ two suns, so that one could feel the/a sun shining on them from two different directions would be pretty weird.

Mike, Mike; you’re supposed to take it romantically, not literally. I get that, but it seems stupid to me all the same. Why am I supposed to change my own perspective just because DV has a whacky/dreamy one?

– Mike Easter
