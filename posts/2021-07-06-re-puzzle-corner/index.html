Today’s CQ:

A creative man is motivated by the desire to achieve, not by the desire to beat others. Ayn Rand

Deciding what to say about AR was a puzzle in itself, since I decided to approach her from the philosopher angle. Her ideas have been embraced by libertarians and conservatives which I would consider myself with trace elements of liberalism.

I read The Fountainhead in hs, presumably my mother’s book, a ‘43 best seller and a ‘49 movie. When I was reading AR’s wp article, I wanted to ‘dissect’ the concept of philosophy, but then I decided I needed to sort the academic disciplines first.

The wp article sorted them into humanities, subdivided sciences into social, natural, formal, and applied. It divided humanities into arts performing and visual, history, languages & literature, law, philosophy & theology.

So, we have the humanities studying human society & culture, while philosophy’s field of study has evolved greatly over the centuries, from ‘all bodies of knowledge’ and a wp article characterizes today’s philosophy study as:

(and other subfields)

So AR became a success w/ The Fountainhead which led to her surrounding herself w/ followers and like-minded and led to Atlas Shrugged, which was her last novel and so-called magnum opus and she was off into her philosopher role of Objectivism, which has its own wp article as it extended from AR in the 50s & 60s and beyond to the present.

There have been numerous books about AR & her philosophy, for and against, including characterizing many followers as ‘cultish’.

– Mike Easter
