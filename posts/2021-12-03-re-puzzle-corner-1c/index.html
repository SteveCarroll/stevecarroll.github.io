When I read the JM wp article, I remembered him being in the news in 1961. There was ‘plenty of’ segregation back in that day; lotsa people in segregated societies were in favor of desegregation, but felt that it was taking place ’too fast’. Naturally if one were being ‘segregated against’ one wouldn’t feel that way. However, there was also a lot of segregation of black and white which was ‘voluntary’ black segregation, perhaps because it was ’easier’ to live in a black community than not.

Those two dates, 1933 dob and 1961 enter Ole Miss, struck me as ‘odd’ (older student) but then I read that JM graduated hs 1951, and went into the USAF for 9 y, which is also a funny number (most people short time as obligated volunteer in those days or ’longer’ career).
