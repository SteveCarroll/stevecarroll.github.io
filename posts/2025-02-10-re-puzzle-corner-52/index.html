Never be limited by other people’s limited imaginations. Mae Jemison

Solving: people’s, never be, by other, limited

MJ has quite a wp bio; she’s done a LOT of different things, an engineering degree, a MD degree, general practice, first black woman astronaut for NASA and then she went into a lot of other unrelated things. I found myself curious about what kind of ‘personality’ she had when I read about her arrest for being uncooperative when she was stopped for an illegal U-turn and the cop was alerted to an outstanding warrant for an unpaid speeding violation and needed to ‘arrest’ her.

I can ‘relate’ to that; many decades ago I was stopped for making a U-turn (in my pickup truck w/ TX plates) by a CA CHP (gravel median on a divided interstate hwy). He ‘bemusedly’ accepted my disclaimer, “It looked like it would be all right to me.” and just gave me a warning.

Another time, while I was parked (near a closed Corvette sales place) someone looking at the ‘vettes on the lot apparently set off an alarm and a cop cruised ‘casing’ the area (and running plates such as someone sitting in a pickup truck w/ a camper) and found that I had outstanding warrants for parking tickets in another city.

I can assure you; if you are encountered or detained or whatever word you want to use and the system has a warrant for your arrest, you had better be cooperative w/ the cop.

I was cooperative, I didn’t go to jail, I was ’taken’ (not handcuffed) to the ‘place’ where the jail and a payment place were and I was able to pay the tickets and the warrants off and get taken back to my truck.

It didn’t go so well for MJ (nor for the cop who arrested her) when she was NOT cooperative w/ a cop who had a warrant against her.

What /kind of/ person doesn’t respect the law/cop when he pretty much HAS to arrest you for an outstanding warrant? Doh.
