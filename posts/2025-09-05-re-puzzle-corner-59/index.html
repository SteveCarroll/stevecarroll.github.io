There are two ways of spreading light: to be the candle or the mirror that reflects it. Edith Wharton

EW’s life was one of affluence; when she started writing ‘in earnest’ in her 40s and subsequently won awards.

The quote is from one of her poems; I like the way this site has interpreted the poem for me:

https://allpoetry.com/Vesalius-In-Zante

The analysis below the poem may be hidden – show analysis.

If one chooses to ‘get into the weeds’ of ‘Vesalius in Zante’ it becomes a very complicated story full of unproven allegations and mysteries. Vesalius was a very important 16th cent anatomist and physician of prominence. One story, related in EW’s poem, is that he dissected an apparently dead person who was still alive; and that further he was subjected to the Inquisition, but that Philip II commuted his sentence to a pilgrimage, and that on the return from the pilgrimage, weather caused his landing on Zante where he died under mysterious circumstances.

This blogger makes much of the story in his investigation https://pampalaia.blogspot.com/2012/03/causes-of-enigmatic-death-of-vesalius.html

I didn’t imagine that the EW quote would go so far.
