Solving: ‘Dr. Suess’ ’this is the day I’

DS was Theodore Suess Geisel; his mom’s maiden name was Suess. I think it’s neat/cool when mothers’ maiden names get to survive. A close male relative got his mother’s maiden name as a middle name and he and his wife gave that middle name to their kid’s middle.

I once ‘proposed’ that when couples marry, they have a hyphenated name composed of her maiden name and his name. (which first? negotiated) Their children would have hyphenated names up until an ‘age of passage’ similar to the ages of bar/bat mitvah or similar ’teens’, and at that time, the child chooses which last name they will take into adulthood. Definitely they ‘can’t’ stay hyphenated.

The other alternative would be that married couple each keep their own names, the children get hyphenated names which they must ‘shed’ by that early-mid teen time in favor of the mother, father, or maybe they get to choose a last name. I haven’t given that last one much tho’t because I lean toward the hyphenated stage, for the couple and the young children in the family, but not the older children.
