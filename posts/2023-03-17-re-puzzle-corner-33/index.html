Mike Easter

March 17, 2023

Today’s CQ:

VXA ANPM INLJDUK FD ZDXOA XTR ANPM ZDXMU FD BSQZU. VXA QNNR BPLJ IPMKPD ANP DXLZ VNMTSTQ XTR TSQZU. SMSKZ FBDKKSTQ

. . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . .

May your pockets be heavy and your heart be light. May good luck pursue you each morning and night. Irish blessing

Solving: ‘Irish’ ‘may your - ‘be’ ‘and’ ‘good’ It would have gone faster if I had recognized ‘blessing’ as quickly as I did ‘Irish’. The OCR gizmo transposed a lot of words.

Sometimes my mother liked to do needlepoint. She gave me one she’d made back in ‘91, ‘May the road rise to meet you. May the wind be always at your back. May the sun shine warm upon your face, the rains fall soft upon your fields and until we meet again may God hold you in the palm of his hand.’ I don’t know how many hours one of those takes to do.

This article’s discussion refutes the premise of the article itself about that blessing:

https://www.irishcentral.com/culture/may-the-road-rise-meet-you-irish-blessing-meaning

… then the .ie poster goes on to explain some things about the original .ie words and what they mean while others dispute some of his points of view.

In any case, I was familiar w/ my mother’s needlepoint ‘road’ blessing, but not the quote.
