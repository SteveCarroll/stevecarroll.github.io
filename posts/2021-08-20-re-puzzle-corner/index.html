Today’s CQ:

Artificial intelligence is devoid of intelligence because it is devoid of artifice. Jean Baudrillard

Solving: It seems to me that I had more trouble w/ that than I should have. I ‘stumbled onto’ ‘intelligence’ fairly early, but somehow I must’ve been making some kind of error because ‘artificial’ didn’t jump out at me at all. I had a hard time figuring out ‘of’ and ‘devoid’. I had recognized the similarity between ‘artifice’ and ‘artificial’ but it was still a problem. And contributing to the problem is that I don’t really ‘get’ Baudrillard.

The wp article on JB (.fr ‘29-‘07) is ’tedious’. There is availability of reading a much larger context for the CQ because JB wrote a number of ’essays’ incl The Transparency of Evil: Essays in Extreme Phenomena several of which are available at GoodReads in which the above quote is available along w/ 350 words of its context, but I didn’t find the quote or its context to be useful to me. I would have no interest in any of JB’s books or essays.
