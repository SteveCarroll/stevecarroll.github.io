All the thoughts of a turtle are turtle. Ralph Waldo Emerson

Solving: all the, thoughts, are, turtle

I observed quite an assortment of misinformation or ‘partial truths’ when I searched on that quote by its source. So I ignored the misinformation and continued to dig for the truth, which turns out to be the RWE wrote a series of essays (which are provided by Project Gutenberg) called Natural history of intellect, and other papers by Ralph Waldo Emerson https://www.gutenberg.org/ebooks/71558

And the larger context of the quote makes more sense than the above quote in ‘isolation’.

There are a number of ‘quaint’ surprises in RWE’s wp article, not the least of how many people near him died of TB, 2 brothers and his first wife who he met when she was 16 married when she was 18 and she died when she was 20.

I’m also puzzled by the fact that he changed his 2nd wife’s first name, called her by several different names, and she called him Mr. Emerson.
