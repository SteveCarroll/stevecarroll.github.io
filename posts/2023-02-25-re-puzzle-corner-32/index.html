Fri NYT xword, a clue/ans only solved by all its crossings: Best-selling Israeli author of ‘Sapiens: A Brief History of Humankind’ Ans: Yuval Noah Harari

neverheardof’im, so I read the articles on the book, which sounded more critical than ‘best selling’, and his article, which was much more positive about his work, and then since I was bemused by the concept of his book having been turned into a ‘graphic novel’, I had to read that article because I’ve never seen one of those. I also found it ‘curious’ that an academically oriented book would be turned into a graphic novel; even tho’ his book critics considered much of his treatment of the topic to be ‘speculative’ and ‘sensational’.

In my schoolboy days, I had access to all the comic books and magazines I could read, so I read them ‘all’ or nearly all; women’s mags, men’s mags, craft mags, hunting & fishing mags, sexology mag, and I could have all of the comic books w/ their covers torn off.

A seller of comic books and mags can get credit for the unsold merch simply by returning the top part of the front cover while discarding the rest, so I got to take home those coverless comics. So I’ve seen a LOT of comics back in my day, but no graphic novels, much less a graphic ’textbook’ of ‘popular science’.
