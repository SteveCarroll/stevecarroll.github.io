Solving: the - and - -ing - on - the stars

There was ‘craziness’ in CK’s story because of the battles between his ex-wife & children and his current wife, before and after his death. There’s some kind of lesson to be learned there, but I’m not sure what it is.

Players:

CK deteriorated. His wife prevented contact between him and the children; they sought conservatorship away from his wife; they failed. Jean took him away from the nursing home; but then Kerri was granted conservatorship and the court ordered an investigation into CK’s whereabouts.

A battle ensued over his medical care during his incompetency because his directive said that he didn’t want to be kept alive and that results in medical disagreements about such as hydration. If you can’t eat or drink and no one gives you IVs, you will die much sooner, and it isn’t as bad a death as one might think.

Then the battle extended over what to do w/ his body. The court had handed it over to Jean who wasn’t going to do anything the family wanted.

The ‘spiriting’ away of the body drags the chapters of the battles on and on. Crazy story.

The family sued Jean for wrongful death, elder abuse, and emotional distress to the children. It took 4 y for the suits to be settled.

BTW he was buried in Oslo, to show us how crazy the battles were.
