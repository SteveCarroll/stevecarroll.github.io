Juneteenth

Growing up in TX, I’ve been around those celebrations as long as I can remember. The story of how that date went from being a ‘Texas-thing’ (the date celebrates the ‘real’ end of slavery in Texas and thus all, the last, of the US states, since TX was the last place to not). It was so ‘proclaimed’ by a union general in Galveston in ‘65.

There are a lot of ‘dates’ for emancipation from Abe Lincoln to congress to constitutional ratifications, but that/this is the one that is celebrated, and only became a federal holiday under the last admin.

There’s a nice juneteenth story by HL Gates the historian and host of a PBS series - wayback is the best way to get it.
