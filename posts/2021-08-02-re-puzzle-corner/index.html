Today’s CQ:

Consistency in regard to opinions is the slow poison of intellectual life. Unknown

Other sources ascribe the quote, w/ some modification to (Sir) Humphry Davy, a significant scientist of the 18-19th century.

Consistency in opinion is the slow poison of intellectual life, the destroyer of its vividness and energy. Humphry Davy

A page full of his alleged quotes has this one I liked:

https://www.quotemaster.org/author/Humphry+Davy

Besides reading about HD’s numerous scientific achievements, I was struck by the reference to him as Cornish (why not British?) so I started reading about the history (and geography + geology) of Cornwall and importantly found a wp article on the culture of Cornwall.

One can get a sense of the SW peninsula from this map of SW England, which shows Cornwall 13, Devon 11, and some other counties. https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5a/SouthWest-local-government-2019.jpg/764px-SouthWest-local-government-2019.jpg
