Today’s CQ:

LGO VRE MPO ZKMMZO CRTTKM PORCV MPO TLE MOZZ PKB GRGGE MPRM PO QRB CORZ. PO QRB LUOCNLXO TE ZLUO KG PKB PORCM. – “MPO UOZUOMOOG CRTTKM”

. . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . .

One day the little rabbit heard the boy tell his nanny that he was real. He was overcome by love in his heart. – “The Velveteen Rabbit”

Solving: ’the’ ’that’ ’little’ ‘rabbit’

That reminds me of The Ghost and Mrs. Muir; when Luci Muir (the lovely Gene Tierney) met Miles Fairley (a 41 y/o George Sanders back in 1947) at the publisher’s (in Whitecliff), but that didn’t turn out as well for LM; as MF was married w/ children back in London. Fortunately, in the end LM and the ghost Daniel Gregg (a seaworthy captain Rex Harrison) go off into ghostly eternity together instead.

While refreshing my recollection of names in the movie’s article, I learned that the early 1900s period scenes of London, the English village of Whitecliff, and the quaint Gull Cottage at English seaside were all shot in central CA US Pacific coastline.
