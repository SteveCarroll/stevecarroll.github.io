One of my problems w/ 'poets' is that they often like to speak
'obliquely' and I don't particularly /like/ to interpret oblique
language. You might think that I should, if I like puzzles, but puzzles
typically play by rules, whereas oblique language is 'free-style' no
rules; like it doesn't HAVE to make sense. I don't particularly like that.

This quote is an example in that it came from this poem:

https://voetica.com/poem/8214

As I read it, I tho't 'yeah yeah waxing poetic' and I didn't really
/care/ if I was interpreting 'correctly' (by some metric). Then when it
concluded w/ 'sexual hair' it jolted me to want to 'understand' or
'translate' the poem better. That was frustrating.

DL's article/bio at the Poetry Foundation is 'better' than the one in
the wp.
