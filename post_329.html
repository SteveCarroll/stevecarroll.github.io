The lovely flowers embarrass me. They make me regret I am not a bee.
Emily Dickinson

Solving: the, regret, embarrass

One of the things I don't like about a lot of poetry is the 'sacrifice'
of perfectly or optimally good 'usage' of language in favor of rhythm
and rhyming.

On the one hand, one can say, "Oh. How poetic." While on the other
might be, "Oh how 'bent' the language."

Embarrass? How so? OK, the flowers are lovely. OK how 'great' it
would be to be a bee and flit and visit the lovely flowers. That's
fine; so you could even 'regret' not being a bee. So, how does regret
being a bee become 'embarrass'? It doesn't. It is all about the
rhythm, not the usage. The usage is wacky. Oh, that's ok because it is
poetic. That makes 'poetic' a dirty word of sorts.
