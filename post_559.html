I didn't know anything about Frozen, except that I had heard of the
movie title, so I read its wp article. I found a lot of interesting
stuff in there, particularly about the dev, which started way back in
'37 before Disney even premiered Snow White. It was (somewhat)
originally a H.C.Andersen tale The Snow Queen (1844).

As one might guess, Olaf was the/a snowman. As well as I can tell Olaf
doesn't even appear in HCA's fairy tale, so he was a Disney writers'
creation 'unrelated' to The Snow Queen story, but was worth billions.

There is a separate wp article for the dev of Olaf and his significance;
all of which is related to the difficulties Disney had in creating a
story that would work for their purposes.
