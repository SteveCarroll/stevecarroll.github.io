If one chooses to 'get into the weeds' of 'Vesalius in Zante' it becomes
a very complicated story full of unproven allegations and mysteries.
Vesalius was a very important 16th cent anatomist and physician of
prominence. One story, related in EW's poem, is that he dissected an
apparently dead person who was still alive; and that further he was
subjected to the Inquisition, but that Philip II commuted his sentence
to a pilgrimage, and that on the return from the pilgrimage, weather
caused his landing on Zante where he died under mysterious circumstances.

This blogger makes much of the story in his investigation
https://pampalaia.blogspot.com/2012/03/causes-of-enigmatic-death-of-vesalius.html
