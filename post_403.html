Toni Morrison

Solving: I, the, have only to, into, and, see summer

I can 'accept' the bending of language about 'breaking into the
tightness of a strawberry' - but I don't like it at all; however I can't
accept 'dust and lowering skies' of summer at all.

This site dev/d a 'discussion' of the liberties that a famous writer
gets - namely a 'pass' on any collection of words she chooses to string
together, but I don't buy that.

I have commented in the past about how important editors are for
writers, whether it be books, short stories, or any other kind of
publication. And, I understand that when you are 'famous' then your
editor would pretty much let you have your way; and I think that is too bad.

In the link, I agree w/ the POV that the quote is 'terrible' and I
disagree w/ the POV that such writers get to say whatever they want to
and it should simply be accepted as 'poetic' or something.
